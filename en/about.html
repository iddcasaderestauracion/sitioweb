More than a church: we are a family

Get to know who we are and what we believe.

Our mission and vision

Mission

Our mission is to obey Jesus Christ's command to go into all the world and preach the gospel of the Kingdom of God, bringing people to the feet of Christ so they may be saved, healed, and restored by the power of the Holy Spirit.

We believe true transformation comes from the Holy Spirit, who sanctifies lives and produces genuine change in the heart.

As a church, we evangelize our community and serve those in greatest need, including widows, orphans, the homeless, and anyone who needs spiritual help and support, reflecting the love and compassion of Christ in action.

Mark 16:15 · Matthew 28:19–20 · John 3:16

Vision

Our vision is for this community to know that we are a church that, by the power of the Holy Spirit, presents a God who truly saves, heals, restores, transforms, and regenerates lives.

We aspire to be a church that positively impacts the society we live in, offering spiritual support, biblical education, and practical tools that help people improve their quality of life.

We envision a church committed to the full formation of leaders, biblically and legally prepared to face the challenges of the present and the future, with the desire to establish our own temple and a certified Bible institute to prepare generations of leaders after God's own heart.

What We Believe

  • In the verbal inspiration of the Bible.
  • In a God who exists eternally in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.
  • That Jesus Christ is the only begotten of the Father, conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary; that He was crucified, buried, and rose from the dead; that He ascended into heaven and is today at the right hand of the Father as our Intercessor.
  • That all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, and that repentance is commanded by God for all and necessary for the forgiveness of sins.
  • That justification, regeneration, and the new birth are accomplished through faith in the blood of Jesus Christ.
  • In sanctification, following the new birth, through faith in the blood of Jesus Christ, by the Word and by the Holy Spirit.
  • That holiness is God's standard of life for His people.
  • In baptism with the Holy Spirit, subsequent to the cleansing of the heart.
  • In speaking in other tongues, as the Spirit leads, which is the initial evidence of the baptism in the Holy Spirit.
  • In water baptism by immersion, and that all who repent should be baptized in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.
  • That divine healing is provided for all in the atonement.
  • In the Lord's Supper and the washing of the saints' feet.
  • In the second coming of Jesus before the millennium: first, to raise the righteous dead and catch up the living saints to meet Him in the air; second, to reign on earth for a thousand years.
  • In the bodily resurrection; eternal life for the righteous and eternal punishment for the wicked.
